WORLD NEWS HEADLINES - JULY 12, 2026

1. GLOBAL WEATHER & NATURAL DISASTERS
- Typhoon Bavi: Eastern China is bracing for Typhoon Bavi, expected to make landfall late Sunday or early Monday. Over 1.7 million people have been evacuated from Zhejiang province, and thousands have been relocated from Shanghai. Before approaching China, the typhoon intensified monsoon rains in the Philippines, causing deadly landslides that left at least 17 people dead.
- Jammu & Kashmir Flash Floods: A cloudburst in the Pahalgam area of Jammu and Kashmir triggered flash floods, inundating several hotels and residential buildings. Tourists have been safely evacuated by local authorities; no casualties have been reported.

2. INTERNATIONAL CONFLICT & DIPLOMACY
- Russia-Ukraine War: Russian forces conducted a massive aerial bombardment across Ukraine with 12 missiles and over 120 drones, killing at least six people and injuring 29. In retaliation, Kyiv claimed a drone strike in the Sea of Azov damaged over two dozen Russian vessels.
- U.S.-Iran Tensions: Iran's Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei, vowed to avenge his father's death. This statement follows warnings from U.S. President Donald Trump that any assassination attempt against him would result in the "decimation" of Iran.

3. BUSINESS & TECHNOLOGY
- Apple vs. OpenAI Lawsuit: Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI, alleging talent poaching and trade secret theft, intensifying the ongoing competition for AI engineering talent.
- OpenAI IPO Preparations: OpenAI is reportedly preparing a confidential IPO filing with major investment banks, aiming for a potential public debut as early as September.
- Gemini 3.5 Pro Release: Google DeepMind is scheduled to launch Gemini 3.5 Pro for general availability on July 17, 2026.
- USPS Stamp Price Increase: The United States Postal Service has increased the price of a first-class stamp from 78 cents to 82 cents, effective today, in response to significant financial losses.

4. OBSERVANCES & AWARENESS
- Malala Day: July 12 is recognized globally to honor Pakistani activist and Nobel laureate Malala Yousafzai.
- International Day of Combating Sand and Dust Storms: Observed today to raise awareness about the environmental and socio-economic hazards of sand and dust storms.
